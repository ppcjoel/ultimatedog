Why Kibble Alone Isn't Enough

The pet food industry has made enormous strides in the last 20 years, but processing is still the Achilles heel. The high-heat extrusion process used to manufacture kibble destroys a significant portion of heat-sensitive nutrients — including probiotics, certain B vitamins, and delicate plant phytonutrients that play a critical role in immune regulation and cellular repair.

Dogs in the wild would naturally consume a varied diet rich in organ meats, fermented plant matter, and fresh whole foods. Domesticated dogs eating the same kibble twice a day, every day, are essentially on a processed-food diet — and their bodies show it. Dull coats, chronic digestive issues, low energy, and premature joint stiffness are among the most common complaints veterinarians hear from owners of otherwise "well-fed" dogs.

Key insight: A 2024 study published in the Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine found that dogs receiving daily whole-food supplementation showed measurable improvements in gut microbiome diversity, inflammatory markers, and coat condition within just 30 days — even when their base diet remained unchanged.
